Low-level laser therapy: innovation in hair loss treatment

Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) is a non-invasive treatment that uses visible red light (630-670 nanometers) to stimulate hair follicles. This advanced technology, also known as photobiomodulation, penetrates the scalp, activating follicular stem cells and increasing microcirculation in the area.

The result is an increase in cellular ATP (adenosine triphosphate) production that revitalizes dormant follicles, prolongs the growth phase of hair, and strengthens existing hair fibers, effectively combating different types of hair loss.

01Stops hair loss: Interrupts the follicular miniaturization process associated with androgenetic alopecia and other forms of hair loss.

Treatment protocol

An initial cycle of 12-16 sessions is recommended, with a frequency of 1-2 times per week, followed by monthly maintenance sessions. Each session lasts approximately 30-45 minutes and is completely painless, allowing you to resume your activities immediately.
